Introduction

In modern web design, creating websites that are both accessible and search engine-friendly is essential. This guide introduces the foundational principles of HTML5 semantic structure, accessibility practices, and SEO basics.

Accessibility Basics

Accessibility ensures that all users, including those using assistive technologies like screen readers, can navigate and understand your content. Use semantic HTML5 elements such as <header>, <nav>, <main>, <section>, and <footer> to provide clear structure.

  • Use descriptive link text (e.g., “Read more about accessibility” instead of “Click here”).
  • Include alt text for images.
  • Ensure proper heading hierarchy (e.g., <h2> under <h1>).

SEO Fundamentals

Search engine optimization (SEO) helps your site rank higher in search engine results. Here are some key practices:

  1. Use meaningful page titles and meta descriptions.
  2. Structure content with headings and subheadings.
  3. Include relevant keywords naturally within your text.

Conclusion

Combining semantic HTML5 with accessibility and SEO best practices results in web pages that are user-friendly, inclusive, and easier to discover online. Start small, and always keep users and clarity at the core of your design.
